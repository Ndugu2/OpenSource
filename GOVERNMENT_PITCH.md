# Proposal: Adoption of the BOSC Community Library
**To:** Ministry of Education
**Subject:** Open Source Software Adoption for National Resource Libraries

## Executive Summary
This proposal advocates for the adoption of the BOSC Community Library over proprietary alternatives for the nation's digital education infrastructure.

## Total Cost of Ownership (TCO)
Proprietary systems incur high recurring licensing fees based on user headcount. The BOSC Community Library, being open-source, requires zero licensing fees. The TCO shifts from recurring capital expenditure (CapEx) to operational expenditure (OpEx) focused purely on hosting, customization, and local IT training. Over a 5-year period, this typically results in a 40-60% reduction in TCO.

## Mitigating Vendor Lock-in
Proprietary vendors often use proprietary data formats, making it extremely expensive and difficult to migrate away from their platforms. By adopting an open-source solution governed by the EUPL, the Ministry retains complete ownership of its data and the underlying code. If a service provider underperforms, the Ministry can simply tender a new contract for support services without having to rebuild the entire system or migrate data.
