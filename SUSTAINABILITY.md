# Sustainability Strategy

## Funding Model Comparison

### The "Red Hat" Service-Based Model
In this model, the BOSC Community Library software remains 100% free and open-source. Revenue is generated by offering enterprise-grade support, certified deployments, training, and SLAs to government departments and NGOs. 
- **Pros:** Aligns with government procurement needs (they buy service contracts, not software licenses). Encourages a high-quality product.
- **Cons:** Requires a dedicated commercial entity to provide these services, which can be difficult to bootstrap.

### The "Foundation-Driven" Model
Under a foundation model, the project is governed by a non-profit consortium (e.g., Apache Software Foundation or a dedicated BOSC Foundation). Funding comes from corporate sponsorships, government grants, and membership fees from stakeholders who want a say in the roadmap.
- **Pros:** Highly transparent, prevents vendor lock-in, and distributes the maintenance burden across multiple stakeholders.
- **Cons:** Decision-making can be slower due to committee governance. Funding can be unpredictable compared to steady service contracts.

**Recommendation:** For the BOSC Community Library, a **hybrid Foundation-driven model** is recommended. The core should be governed by a neutral non-profit to ensure public trust, while a marketplace of private vendors can provide "Red Hat"-style support services.
