# Reflective Journal: Governance and Hostile Forks

As the Lead Community Maintainer for the BOSC Community Library, establishing clear governance has been the most critical aspect of the project. The decision to use a robust license (EUPL) and a clear Code of Conduct sets the baseline for interactions.

I would ensure that the main repository's roadmap is clear and heavily influenced by the needs of our primary stakeholders (the public sector). If the hostile fork attempts to re-brand using our trademarks, I would enforce our trademark policy. However, regarding the code, they are legally permitted to fork it under the EUPL. The best defense against a hostile fork is maintaining high community trust, transparent decision-making, and a welcoming environment for external contributors.
